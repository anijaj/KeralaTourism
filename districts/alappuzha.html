Alappuzha

Alappuzha, also known by its former name Alleppey, is the administrative headquarters of Alappuzha District in the Indian State of Kerala. Alappuzha is a city and a municipality in Kerala and ranks third among the districts in literacy rate in the State of Kerala. In 2016, the Centre for Science and Environment rated Alappuzha as the cleanest town in India. Fondly called as the 'Venice of the East', Alappuzha is the ultimate backwater destination of Kerala. Previously this destination was referred to as Alleppey. The great Lake of Vembanad and the smaller canals here are the perfect spot to enjoy a slow cruise. This is also the main reason for the boom in the number of houseboats, which now almost touches a figure of 1200. One night cruise on the backwaters of the Alappuzha is one of the most popular cruises offered. A round trip, in which you board the boat at Alappuzha and disembark at Alappuzha after having spent the night in the tranquility of the Backwaters.
